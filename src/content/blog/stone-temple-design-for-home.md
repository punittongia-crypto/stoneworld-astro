<h2><b>Care &amp; Maintenance of Stone Mandirs</b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A stone mandir is not just a spiritual centrepiece; it is also an investment in timeless beauty. To ensure that your mandir continues to radiate divinity and elegance for years, proper care and maintenance are essential. Fortunately, natural stone is known for its durability and requires only minimal upkeep.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Regular cleaning with a soft, damp cloth helps preserve the natural shine of marble, granite, or sandstone mandirs. For daily care, avoid using harsh chemical cleaners, as they may damage the surface or dull the finish. Instead, opt for mild soap solutions or stone-safe cleaners that gently remove dust and residue. In the case of carved temples, dusting with a soft brush ensures intricate details remain free from dirt buildup.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Polished marble mandirs may benefit from occasional re-polishing to maintain their radiant glow, while granite temples retain their lustre with simple wiping and sealing if required. Sandstone mandirs, with their porous texture, should be protected from excessive moisture to prevent staining, making periodic sealing a good option.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">With these simple care practices, your </span><b><a href="https://stoneworld.co.in/blog/which-type-of-mandir-is-good-for-home/">stone mandir</a> will remain a sacred, pristine, and enduring part of your home for generations.</b></p>

<h2><b>FAQs</b></h2>
<h4><strong>Which stone is best for a home mandir?</strong></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">White marble is the most popular choice for its purity and elegance. Granite offers durability and modern appeal, while sandstone provides traditional charm with earthy tones. Each stone suits different styles and preferences.</span></p>
<h4><b>How do I choose the right stone temple design for my home?</b></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Consider your space, interior style, and Vastu principles. Wall-mounted temples are great for compact homes, while floor-standing carved designs suit larger spaces. Materials like marble or granite enhance elegance and longevity.</span></p>
<h4><b>Can a stone mandir be customised?</b></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Yes. At Stone World, we create custom stone mandirs based on size, carvings, finishes, and stone type. This allows homeowners to design a temple that perfectly matches their interior style and spiritual requirements.</span></p>
<h4><b>How do I maintain a stone temple at home?</b></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Stone mandirs are low-maintenance. Regular dusting and wiping with a damp cloth keep them clean. Avoid harsh chemicals; use mild soap or stone-safe cleaners. Occasional polishing or sealing preserves shine and durability.</span></p>
<h4><b>Where should I place a stone mandir at home?</b></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">According to Vastu Shastra, the northeast (Ishanya) corner is the most auspicious place for a pooja mandir. Avoid placing it under staircases or in bedrooms. Proper placement enhances spiritual energy.</span></p>
<h4><b>Are stone mandirs suitable for modern interiors?</b></h4>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Absolutely. Stone temples can be designed with sleek, minimal carvings for contemporary homes or with traditional detailing for classic interiors. Marble and granite mandirs especially blend well with modern décor while maintaining sacredness.</span></p>
